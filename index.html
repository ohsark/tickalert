Tick Alert
A Surveillance System to keep track of ticks amongst dogs in Autralia.
Ixodes holocyclus and I. cornuatus are endemic tick species responsible for the debilitating and often fatal condition in companion animals known as tick paralysis. Symptoms include loss of appetite, poor mobility and coordination, respiratory problems and death. Both of the causative tick species are distributed along Australia’s east coast and are associated with wet and humid habitats. These distributions coincide with some of Australia’s most populous centres. Encroachment of suburbs into bushland is expected to increase future risks of tick paralysis.Prevention of bites by paralysis ticks relies on mass use of acaricide drugs, equating to millions of dollars spent each year. These require regular application (for topical ointments) or administration and there is growing concern that they are not completely effective. Avoidance of tick-prone areas, particularly during periods of heightened infestation risk, remains a critical control measure.
