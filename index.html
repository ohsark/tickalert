TickAlert: Forecasting canine tick paralysis admissions to Australian veterinary clinics
Ixodes holocyclus and I. cornuatus are endemic tick species responsible for the debilitating condition in companion animals known as tick paralysis. Symptoms include loss of appetite, poor mobility, respiratory problems and sometimes death. Prevention of bites by paralysis ticks relies on mass use of acaricide drugs, equating to millions of dollars spent each year, yet presentation to veterinary clinics with tick paralysis remains a common issue for Australian dogs. The purpose of this platform is to use data on tick paralysis admissions to Australian veterinary clinics (relying on the VetCompass database) to understand why some seasons are worse than others and to provide informed predictions about what coming tick seasons may look like.
