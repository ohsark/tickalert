TickAlert: Forecasting canine tick paralysis admissions to Australian veterinary clinics
Tick paralysis is a severe disease of companion animals characterised by flaccid paralysis caused by a neurotoxin produced by feeding ticks. In Australia, Ixodes holocyclus and I. cornuatus are endemic tick species responsible for the condition.

Even though bites from paralysis ticks can be effectively reduced with the use of acaricide drugs, tick paralysis presentations to Australian veterinary clinics remains a common issue for our companion animals.

The TickAlert platform uses real-time data on tick paralysis admissions across Australian primary care veterinary clinics registered to the VetCompass Australia Consortium. The TickAlert platform provides data-driven national and local tick paralysis patterns and forecasts for users to understand the current risk for their pets and anticipate the trajectory of the upcoming tick paralysis season.
